key Concepts

Computers are everywhere – A look today’s computer options

Personal Computers (PC) - A computer for your everyday needs, choose between desktop-based or portable.

  • Desktop computers: Like its name, it is a computer intended to be used on a desk. It is usually bigger than portable computers and often has more flexibility for hardware updates (although many portable computers allow for updates). Desktop computers may also be more cost-effective if you are looking for more power, such as what is required for playing many computer games.
  • Portable Computers: Just like its name, it is a computer made to be easily transported. Comes in many different sizes:

Some options for portable computers are Laptop and Tablet computers.

* A laptop computer is usually the bigger of the portable devices and usually more potent. Because of its potential for hardware updates, it is also the most comparable to desktop computers.

* Tablet computers can be the same size as laptop computers or smaller. Unlike laptop or desktop computers, it does not have a keyboard and mouse or touchpad as it uses a touchscreen. A touchscreen is optimally used with a stylus but can also be used with fingers.

Mobile Devices

  • Mobile devices are small, portable devices used as computers or to access the internet. Today, the most popular mobile devices are phones. Many computer users rely on their phones for everyday computing needs such as emailing, reading webpages or playing games. These phones are often called “ smartphones.”
  • Other mobile devices for internet usage and other computing needs are smartwatches, tablets, e-readers and many others.

Embedded Computers

  • A type of computer that may not come to mind when you think of a computer are embedded computers. Embedded computers are tiny computers embedded into devices to perform a specific task. They can be found in common kitchen appliances such as air fryers and microwaves, gym equipment such as ellipticals or stair climbers, and vehicles such as the devices inside mirrors to alert drivers of a blind spot.

For the curious minds – Other important computers:

Servers

  • A computer is used to host programs and data for a small network.
  • Usually bigger, more powerful and expensive than desktop computers.
  • Frequently used in schools and small businesses but also available for home use.

Mainframe computers

  • Powerful computers are used by large organizations such as universities, hospitals, banks and government agencies.
  • More powerful than servers
  • Manage large amounts of centralized data
  • Usually located in secured, climate-controlled data centres within the organization it belongs to.

Supercomputers

  • Used by applications that need high precision, speed, power and processing abilities.
  • Most power and expensive computer available.
  • Very large computers with a vast amount of CPUs
