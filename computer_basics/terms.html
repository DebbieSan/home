basic computer terms

  1. Application software. Programs that enable users to perform specific tasks on a computer, such as writing letters or playing games; also called apps.
  2. Computer. A programmable, electronic device that accepts data input, performs processing operations on that data, and output and stores results.
  3. Computer network. A collection of computers and other hardware devices that are connected together to share hardware, software, and data as well as to communicate electronically with one another.
  4. Data. Raw, unorganized facts
  5. Desktop computer. A personal computer designed to fit on or nest to a desk.
  6. Embedded computer. A tiny computer embedded in a product and designed to perform specific tasks or functions for that product.
  7. Hardware. The physical parts of a computer system, such as the keyboard, monitor, printer and so forth.
  8. Information. Data that has been processed into a meaningful form.
  9. Input: The process of entering data into a computer, can also refer to the data itself.
  10. Internet. The largest and most well-known computer network, linking millions of computers all over the world.
  11. Internet appliance. A device that is used to access the Internet.
  12. Mainframe computer. A computer used in large organizations (such as hospitals, large businesses, and colleges) that need to manage large amounts of centralized data and run multiple programs simultaneously.
  13. Media tablet. A mobile device, usually larger than a smartphone, that is typically used to access the internet and display multimedia content.
  14. Mobile device. A very small device that has built-in computing or internet capability.
  15. Notebook. A fully functioning portable computer that opens to reveal a screen and keyboard; also called a laptop computer.
  16. Output. The process of presenting the results of processing, can also refer to the results themselves.
  17. Personal computer (PC). A type of computer based on a microprocessor and designed to be used by one person at a time; also called a microcomputer.
  18. Portable Computer. A small, personal computer, such as a notebook or tablet, that is designed to be carried around easily.
  19. Processing: Performing operations on data that has been input into a computer to convert that input to output.
  20. Server. A computer used to host programs and data for a small network.
  21. Smartphone. A mobile device based on a mobile phone that includes internet capabilities and can run mobile apps.
  22. Software. The instructions, also called computer programs, that are used to tell a computer what it should do.
  23. Storage. The operation of saving data programs or output for future use.
  24. Supercomputer. The faster, most expensive, and most powerful type of computer.
  25. Tablet computer. A portable computer about the size of a notebook that is designed to be used with a digital pen.
